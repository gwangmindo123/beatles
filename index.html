The Beatles' History

  • The Quarrymen

    The Beatles' story begins in Liverpool, England, in the late 1950s. John Lennon formed a skiffle group called The Quarrymen. Paul McCartney and George Harrison joined later. After a few name changes, they became The Beatles in 1960.

    The Quarrymen in 1957

  • Beatlemania

    By 1963, The Beatles had become a worldwide phenomenon. Their catchy pop songs, charismatic personalities, and distinctive haircuts sparked a level of fan frenzy never seen before, a phenomenon dubbed "Beatlemania." They conquered America in 1964, starting the "British Invasion."

    The Beatles arriving in the US in 1964

  • The Studio Years

    From 1966 onwards, The Beatles stopped touring and focused on studio recording. This period saw them produce some of their most innovative and influential work, including the albums "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" and "Abbey Road." The band officially broke up in 1970.

    The Beatles in the studio
